For every year since 1929, a select group of Hollywood insiders has met

to recognize outstanding achievements in the film industry.

This committee, known as The Academy, gives out a series of annual awards

known as Academy Awards, or Oscars.

Among these awards, the most prestigious honor a film can receive

is the Academy Award for Best Picture.

Studio Breakdown

In the early years of the Oscars, highly coveted Best Picture wins and nominations were dominated by "The Big Five" studios. Today, Academy Award Wins come from a wider variety of studios.

Genre Breakdown

While movies of any genre can be nominated for best picture, dramas are nominated disproportionately for the ceremony's highest honor.

Budget and Box Office

One way to assess audience assessment of a movie is through box office performance, as the movie-going audience deems them worthy of seeing. For each year, given data availability, we wanted to see whether the movie with the highest box office won the Best Picture Academy Award most often, or if the movie with the highest budget, and thus the greatest spectacle, won Best Picture most often.
